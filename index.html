President of Fisher Hall

When I am on campus but not working on my schoolwork, I am generally in or around Fisher Hall. While Notre Dame does not have any greek life on campus, our dorms are single-sex, and we stay in these dorms for 3-4 years. We come to associate with our dorm, and we form a tremendous brotherhood. The men I have been fortunate enough to meet in Fisher are amazing people, and I am very proud to be representing them as their president.

As president, I have many responsibilities, two of which include participating in Hall Presidents Council and planning major events for the dorm. With a staff of over 20 commissioners, my Vice President Andy Miles and I are able to plan 2 signature events and many miscellaneous events throughout the year. Our biggest event is the Fisher Regatta, which defines life in Fisher throughout the year.

Relevant Coursework:

Sophomore Year:

  • Fundamentals of Computing I: Learned the C language's concepts such as arrays, functions, and pointers, along with techniques and algorithms for approaching and solving problems in computer science
  • Discrete Math: Introduction to theoretical computer science concepts, including logic, set theory, recursion, algorithms, and graphs
  • Fundamentals of Computing II: Learned more advanced OOP concepts in C++ such as classes/objects, polymorphism, and inheritance, along with techniques for developing large software systems
  • Basic Unix: Learned how to navigate a Unix environment, including shell scripting in bash and tcsh, makefiles, vim, and scripting languages Perl and Python
  • Logic Design: Learned concepts of boolean algebra and switching circuits, Karnaugh maps, design of combinational and of sequential logic networks, and sequential machines

Junior Year:

  • Data Structures: Fundamental techniques in the design and analysis of non-numerical algorithms and their data structures: lists, stacks, queues, priority queues and search trees
  • Compilers: An introduction to the fundamental techniques and tools used in compiler construction, including language specification, lexical analysis, parsing techniques, run-time environments, and code generation.
  • Computer Architecture: Introduction to architectural concepts present in current scalar machines, assembly language programming (MIPS), and computer arithmetic
  • Database Concepts: Learned effective techniques in managing, retrieving and updating information from a database, query processing, distributed databases, and data security
  • Computer Graphics: Learned about computer graphics, including two-and three-dimensional geometries, raster graphics algorithms, primitive rendering, 3-D modeling, and scene description
