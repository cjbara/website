President of Fisher Hall

There are not many things that I am as passionate about as Fisher Hall. When I am on campus and not working on my schoolwork, I am generally in or around Fisher. While Notre Dame does not have greek life on campus, our dorms are single-sex, and most students reside in these dorms for 3-4 years. We come to associate with our dorm, and we form a tremendous brotherhood. Fisher has become my family away from home, and I have a tremendous sense of pride about my dorm. The men I have been fortunate enough to meet in Fisher are amazing people, and I am very proud to be representing them this year as their president.

As president, I have many responsibilities, two of which include participating in Hall Presidents Council and planning major events for the dorm. With a staff of over 20 commissioners, my Vice President Andy Miles and I are able to plan 2 signature events and many weekly activities throughout the year. Our flagship event is the Fisher Regatta, which defines life in Fisher, even when there are still.

I love to lead and set a good example for those who look up to me. As a Junior this year, I have been able to help the new Fishermen adapt to campus life and make their initial college experience a great one. Hopefully my example will carry on to those under me, and inspire them to do great things in the future.

Every year, the men of Fisher sport what is known as a "Regattahawk," a mohawk that simultaneously raises money for charity and also shows dorm pride. In order to lead my brothers, I felt compelled to get a very nice Regattahawk (right). This year's proceeds were donated to St. Adalbert's, a poor Catholic school and parish in South Bend with whom the men of Fisher have been working for a year now. Overall, we raised $5,100 at the Regatta last year to help purchase a new gym floor for the school.

My Regattahawk looked very good this year. Even my sister Lauren (left) came out to support the Fisher Regatta last year.

Relevant Coursework

Sophomore Year:

  • Fundamentals of Computing I: Learned the C language's concepts such as arrays, functions, and pointers, along with techniques and algorithms for approaching and solving problems in computer science
  • Discrete Math: Introduction to theoretical computer science concepts, including logic, set theory, recursion, algorithms, and graphs
  • Fundamentals of Computing II: Learned more advanced OOP concepts in C++ such as classes/objects, polymorphism, and inheritance, along with techniques for developing large software systems
  • Basic Unix: Learned how to navigate a Unix environment, including shell scripting in bash and tcsh, makefiles, vim, and scripting languages Perl and Python
  • Logic Design: Learned concepts of boolean algebra and switching circuits, Karnaugh maps, design of combinational and of sequential logic networks, and sequential machines

Junior Year:

  • Data Structures: Fundamental techniques in the design and analysis of non-numerical algorithms and their data structures: lists, stacks, queues, priority queues and search trees
  • Compilers: An introduction to the fundamental techniques and tools used in compiler construction, including language specification, lexical analysis, parsing techniques, run-time environments, and code generation.
  • Computer Architecture: Introduction to architectural concepts present in current scalar machines, assembly language programming (MIPS), and computer arithmetic
  • Database Concepts: Learned effective techniques in managing, retrieving and updating information from a database, query processing, distributed databases, and data security
  • Computer Entrepreneurship: Learned about many different computer startups, analyzed how they have succeeded or why they have failed, and the strategies for being a leader/entrepreneur of a tech startup.
